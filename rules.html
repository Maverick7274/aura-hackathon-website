Hackathon Rules and Regulations

Terminologies

  • Hackathon – A competition between several teams proposing a solution based on applications of IT technology to various social / community issues / challenges. The competition runs for 5 days, during which the participating teams try to bring the project (software solution) to an advanced stage of development. At the end of the competition, the best solutions presented will be awarded.
  • Organizer – The organizing body of the hackathon competition is Fusion Tech Club.
  • Partner – The entities involved in the smooth running of the event, including but not limited to: sponsors; institutional partners; partner communities; collaborators; promoters; media partners; technical partners, other entities that contribute to the hackathon.
  • Matchmaking – A process that facilitates contact between several people to form a team to participate in the hackathon competition.
  • Hackathon team – A team of two or more people, usually with complementary skills and knowledge, entered in a hackathon competition.
  • Mentor – A person with professional knowledge in technology and / or other sectors of activity relevant to the theme / themes of the competition and who, during its development, offers advice / guidance to a team (or several) entered in the competition.
  • Jury – A decision-making body composed of several people with prestige / professional recognition who attend the presentations of the teams entered in the competition and, based on an evaluation procedure (grids, methodologies, etc.) establishes a ranking of them for the award.
  • Challenge – Is a competition theme pre-established by the hackathon organizers for which the registered teams must propose possible ideas / projects / solutions of a technical nature that will contribute to the digitization of public authorities and institutions. A hackathon contest can have one or more such challenges.
  • Solution – In the context of a hackathon contest by “solution” is meant a complex of measures that address a problem in order to solve / improve it. The solution will be based on a programming and / or other technological technology.

Eligibility & Requirements

  • Participants from ages 17 - 25 are eligible for the event.
  • The hackathon is not open to members of the committee. However, they might help the mentors of the teams during the coding/development stage.
  • Participants are required to bring their Laptops and their Chargers.
  • Participant should have a basic knowledge of Computers, and if you are new to programming you can use our resource page for getting an idea here.

Team Formation

  • Teams can consist of at least 1 team member(solo) to a maximum of four members.
  • Changes to team members are not permitted

Project Development

  • No development may start before the actual date and time of the event. Any teams that violate this rule will be automatically disqualified.
  • The Team is required to submit a video of their project before the deadline. The video should be of maximum 5 minutes and should contain the following:
    • A brief introduction of the team members
    • A brief introduction of the project
    • A demo of the project

Project Submission

  • All teams should have a team name.
  • All the Teams should be registered as a GitHub Organization in GitHub
  • The team should have a GitHub repository for the project.
  • The team should have a README.md file in the repository.
  • There should be a link to the live demo of the project in the README.md file.
  • There should be a License under MIT License in the repository.
  • The team should have a LICENSE.md file in the repository.

Prizes

  • The winning team will be awarded with a cash prize of Rs. 20,000/-.
  • The Second team will be awarded with a cash prize of Rs. 10,000/-.
  • The Third team will be awarded with a cash prize of Rs. 5,000/-.
  • Every team will be awarded with a certificate of participation.

Attendee Code of Conduct

  • All attendees, speakers, sponsors and volunteers at our event are required to agree with the following code of conduct. Organizers will enforce this code throughout the event. We are expecting cooperation from all participants to help ensuring a safe environment for everybody.
  • Be Respectful Be kind to others. Do not insult or put down other attendees. Behave professionally. Remember that harassment and racist, sexist, or exclusionary jokes are not appropriate for this event*. *Harassment includes offensive verbal comments related to gender, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, religion, sexual images in public spaces, deliberate intimidation, stalking, following, harassing photography or recording, sustained disruption of talks or other events, inappropriate physical contact, and unwelcome sexual attention.
  • Be Considerate Attendees violating these rules may be asked to leave the event at the sole discretion of the event organizers without a refund of any charge that may have been levied.
  • Be Thoughtful In the spirit of education and inclusiveness, there may be minors participating in the hackathon. Keep this in mind when speaking.
  • Be Respectful of the Venue and Other Attendees The venue and all facilities are the property of the hackathon organizers. Please treat them with respect.
  • Be Open We welcome attendees from all backgrounds. This event is about increasing the overall amount of learning, partnership, and engagement in the developer community.
  • Be Aware of Your Surroundings Be aware of your surroundings and of your fellow attendees. Alert event staff if you notice a dangerous situation, someone in distress, or violations of this Code of Conduct, even if they seem inconsequential.
  • Be Awesome Do your best and believe in yourself. Participants violating these rules may be asked to leave the hackathon at the sole discretion of the organizers. Thank you for helping make this a welcoming, friendly place for all.

Evaluation

  • There will be Three Rounds in Total :
    • Round 1 : Idea Presentaion
    • Round 2 : Project Presentation
    • Round 3 : Project Submission
  • The projects will be evaluated based on the following criteria:
    • Idea
      • Is the idea unique?
      • Is the idea feasible?
      • Is the idea innovative?
    • Design
      • Is the design user friendly?
      • Is the design appealing?
      • Is the design responsive?
    • Implementation
      • Is the implementation of the project good?
      • Is the implementation of the project efficient?
      • Is the implementation of the project effective?
    • Presentation
      • Is the presentation of the project good?
      • Is the presentation of the project effective?
      • Is the presentation of the project efficient?
    • Tech Stack
      • Is the Tech Stack Choosen good?
      • Is the Tech Stack Efficient?
      • Is the Tech Stack Appropriate for the Problem Statement?
